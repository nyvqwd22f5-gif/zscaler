# About Intermediate CA Certificates
Source: https://help.zscaler.com/zia/about-intermediate-ca-certificates
PDF: https://help.zscaler.com/pdf/com/en/1402626.pdf

You can use the Intermediate CA Certificates page to manage your custom or default intermediate CA certificate for SSL Inspection.
The Intermediate CA Certificates provide the following benefits and enable you to:
- Protect the highly sensitive root CA certificate.
- Scale the organization certificate issuance processes effectively.
- Add an additional layer of security.
- Create a multi-tiered certificate chain, making certificate management more structured and easier to manage.
**About the Intermediate CA Certificates Page**
On the Intermediate CA Certificates page (Policy > SSL Inspection), you can do the following:
- [Add an intermediate CA certificate](/zia/choosing-ca-certificate-ssl-inspection).
- View a list of all intermediate CA certificates. For each intermediate CA certificate (Software Protection or Cloud HSM Protection), you can view:
- **Name**: The name of the certificate.
- **Protection Type**: The protection type of the certificate.
- **Region**: The region of the certificate.
- **Status**: The status of the certificate (enabled or disabled).
- **Validity Start Date**: The date from which the certificate is valid.
- **Expiration Date**: The expiration date of the certificate.
- **Description**: Additional notes or information about the certificate.
- [Modify the table and its columns](/zia/using-tables).
- [Edit or delete a custom intermediate CA certificate.](/zia/editing-deleting-duplicating-items)
You cannot delete a draft or the default intermediate CA certificate.
- [Edit and view the Zscaler Intermediate CA Certificate.](/zia/choosing-ca-certificate-ssl-inspection#Zscaler-Intermediate-Certificate)
- Go to the [SSL Inspection Policy page](/zia/about-ssl-inspection-policy).
