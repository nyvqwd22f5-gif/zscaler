# Deleting Disconnected App Connectors
Source: https://help.zscaler.com/zpa/deleting-disconnected-app-connectors
PDF: https://help.zscaler.com/pdf/com/en/1485216.pdf

Zscaler recommends that you evaluate your service configuration to confirm there is no service outage before deleting disconnected App Connectors.
This article describes how to delete disconnected App Connectors.
To delete a disconnected App Connector:
- Go to **Configuration & Control** > **Private Infrastructure** > **App Connector Management** > **App Connectors**.
- In the filters drop-down menu, click **Add Filters** and select the **Disconnected** filter.
[See image.](#disconnected)
- Select the amount of time in the drop-down menu based on the amount of time the App Connector has been disconnected (e.g., **14 days**, **30 days**, **60 days**, or **90 days**).
- Click **Apply**. After the filter is applied, checkboxes appear next to the App Connectors in the table. You may select individual App Connectors or click the checkbox next to **Name **in the table to select all App Connectors.
[See image.](#checkboxes)
- Select **Delete **from the **Actions** drop-down menu.
[See image.](#delete)
A confirmation message appears.
[See image.](#confirmDelete)
- Select the checkbox to confirm the deletion, then click **Delete**.
